The JLPT N4 is the intermediate level of the Japanese Language Proficiency Test, covering roughly 1,500 vocabulary items, 200 kanji, and basic grammar structures. It bridges the gap between beginner (N5) and upper-intermediate (N3). At this level, you can understand simple conversations about daily topics, read short passages with some kanji support, and handle most situations encountered in everyday life in Japan.

**Study tips:** Review vocabulary daily using spaced repetition (Anki, WaniKani). Focus on common kanji compounds rather than isolated kanji. Read simple native material like manga or graded readers to reinforce words in context. Practice listening with Japanese media aimed at learners before moving to authentic content.
