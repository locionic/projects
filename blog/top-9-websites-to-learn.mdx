Free online courses are great, but let's be real — sometimes you just want something tangible to show for all that effort. A certificate you can actually put on your LinkedIn or résumé does the trick nicely.

The good news? You don't have to pay an arm and a leg for them. Here's where to find free (or mostly free) online courses that come with certificates.

## 1. LinkedIn Learning (formerly Lynda.com)

LinkedIn Learning is a solid all-rounder. Since it got absorbed into LinkedIn, the certificates you earn there actually link nicely to your professional profile — which is pretty convenient.

**Topics covered:** Cloud Computing, Data Science, Marketing, Finance, DevOps, IT, Web Development, Graphic Design, Photography, Project Management, CAD, Video Editing, Mobile App Development, Leadership, and way more.

**Best for:** People who want to beef up their LinkedIn profile while learning job-ready skills.

---

## 2. Alison

Alison's been around forever and has one of the biggest collections of free courses out there. Their certificates won't break the bank (yes, there are free and paid tiers), and the platform covers a genuinely wide range of subjects.

**Topics covered:** Business, IT, Health, Languages, Math, Science, Humanities, Marketing, Design, Web Development.

**Best for:** Learners who want variety and don't mind navigating some upsells to get the free certificate.

---

## 3. Microsoft Learn

If you're looking to level up with Microsoft tools — Azure, Power Platform, Dynamics — this is basically the official training ground. Microsoft Learn is completely free and designed to prep you for their certification exams.

**Topics covered:** Administration, AI Engineering, Business Analysis, Data Engineering, Solution Architecture, Web Development.

**Best for:** Aspiring cloud professionals and anyone targeting Microsoft certifications.

---

## 4. Udemy

Udemy runs sales constantly, so "paid" is relative — you can often snag courses for cheap. Their certificate of completion isn't accredited, but it still looks decent on a résumé and shows initiative.

**Topics covered:** AWS, Python, Java, digital marketing, blockchain, Excel, Linux, Salesforce, Dropshipping, video editing, iOS development, and pretty much anything else you can think of.

**Best for:** Casual learners who stumble upon a good deal and want structured video content.

---

## 5. Oxford Home Study Centre

Oxford Home Study offers free courses in business and management topics. The certificates look professional, and the curriculum is straightforward.

**Topics covered:** Business Management, HR, Project Management, Hotel Management, Construction Management, Fashion Design, Internet Marketing, Supply Chain Management, Tourism.

**Best for:** People in hospitality, business, or management roles looking for quick credentials.

---

## 6. Open Learning

Open Learning takes a university-style approach to online learning. They partner with actual institutions to bring accredited courses to the masses — some free to audit, some paid.

**Topics covered:** Blockchain, Design, English, Cybersecurity, Japanese, AR & VR, Global Business, Understanding Coffee, Ecotourism.

**Best for:** Learners who want a more academic feel without going to grad school.

---

## 7. Pluralsight

Pluralsight is a favorite among developers and IT pros. Built around tech skills with a strong emphasis on hands-on learning. They do have a free tier, though the full library requires a subscription.

**Topics covered:** Java, Python, JavaScript, C#, Angular, Microsoft Azure, IT Networking, SQL, Cybersecurity, AI, Machine Learning, Cloud Computing, Android.

**Best for:** Developers and IT professionals looking to stay current with tech.

---

## 8. freeCodeCamp

freeCodeCamp started with coding, but it's grown into something bigger. All courses are completely free, and their certifications carry real weight in developer communities.

**Topics covered:** Web Development, Data Science, Machine Learning, Business Communication, Big Data, Productivity.

**Best for:** Beginners to中级 developers who want completely free, no-strings-attached learning.

---

## 9. Google Digital Garage

Google Digital Garage is Google's official free training platform. Their certificates are issued directly by Google — which makes them pretty eye-catching on any profile.

**Topics covered:** Digital Marketing, Business Security, Productivity, Machine Learning, Business Communication.

**Best for:** Marketers, entrepreneurs, and anyone who wants Google-branded credentials.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Microsoft Learn** and **Google Digital Garage** offer genuinely free certificates from recognizable brands.
- **LinkedIn Learning** certificates sync directly to your LinkedIn profile — convenient for job seekers.
- **freeCodeCamp** and **Alison** are the best completely free options if you're on a tight budget.
- **Pluralsight** and **Udemy** shine for tech and developer skills, but lean more toward paid content.
- Always check whether a "free" course requires payment to unlock the certificate — read the fine print.

## FAQ

**Are certificates from these platforms actually free?**

Some platforms offer free certificates, others let you audit courses for free but charge for certificates. Microsoft Learn, Google Digital Garage, and freeCodeCamp are among the most genuinely free options.

**Do employers recognize free online course certificates?**

It depends on the employer and the platform. Certificates from well-known brands (Google, Microsoft, LinkedIn) carry more weight than lesser-known platforms. That said, any certificate shows initiative and a willingness to learn — which hiring managers often appreciate.

**What's the difference between a certificate of completion and an accredited certificate?**

A certificate of completion just proves you finished the course. An accredited certificate meets educational standards and may be recognized by professional bodies. Most free online platforms offer certificates of completion, not accredited ones.

**Can I put these certificates on my LinkedIn?**

Yes — LinkedIn Learning certifies directly to your LinkedIn profile. For other platforms, you can add certificates manually under the "Licenses & Certifications" section of your profile.

**Which platform is best for learning coding?**

freeCodeCamp for completely free web development, Pluralsight for paid tech depth, and Microsoft Learn for cloud and Azure-specific skills.

**Do I need any prior experience to start?**

Most introductory courses assume zero prior knowledge. Check the course description — they'll usually indicate if a course is beginner, intermediate, or advanced.

**Can I get a certificate without completing 100% of a course?**

Usually no. Most platforms require you to finish all modules and pass any assessments before issuing a certificate. Some allow free auditing without certification.
