## Introduction

You've written great blog posts — but how do you know if anyone is finding them on Google? **Google Search Console** (GSC) is a free tool from Google that shows you exactly how your site performs in search results.

In this guide, you'll learn how to set up GSC and use it to grow your blog traffic.

---

## 1. Setting Up Google Search Console

1. Go to **search.google.com/search-console**
2. Click **Add Property**
3. Enter your domain (e.g. `locionic.com`)
4. Verify ownership via DNS TXT record (add to Namecheap)
5. Click **Verify**

Once verified, GSC will start collecting data about your site.

---

## 2. Submit Your Sitemap

Submitting your sitemap helps Google find all your pages faster:

1. Click **Sitemaps** in the left sidebar
2. Enter `sitemap.xml`
3. Click **Submit**

Google will crawl your site and index your pages within a few days.

---

## 3. Understanding the Performance Report

The **Performance** tab is the most important section. It shows:

- **Total clicks** — how many people clicked your site from Google
- **Total impressions** — how many times your site appeared in search results
- **Average CTR** — click-through rate (clicks ÷ impressions)
- **Average position** — your average ranking position

### What to look for:
- Pages with **high impressions but low CTR** — your title or description needs improvement
- Pages with **position 5–15** — these are close to page 1 and worth optimizing
- **Queries** tab — see exactly what keywords people use to find you

---

## 4. Improving Low CTR Pages

If a page has many impressions but few clicks, improve your title and meta description:
```tsx
// In your Next.js page
export const metadata = {
  title: 'How to Write Better Unit Tests in JavaScript | Locionic',
  description: 'Learn practical techniques to write cleaner unit tests in JavaScript using Jest. Improve code quality and catch bugs early.',
}
```

A good title should:
- Include your target keyword
- Be under 60 characters
- Be compelling enough to click

---

## 5. Using URL Inspection

The **URL Inspection** tool lets you check any page on your site:

1. Paste a URL into the search bar at the top
2. See if it's indexed by Google
3. Click **Request Indexing** for new or updated pages

Use this every time you publish a new blog post to get it indexed faster.

---

## 6. Fixing Coverage Issues

The **Coverage** report shows pages Google couldn't index. Common issues:

- **404 errors** — broken links, fix or redirect them
- **Soft 404s** — pages that return 200 but have no content
- **Blocked by robots.txt** — check your `robots.txt` file

---

## 7. Tracking Growth Over Time

Set a reminder to check GSC once a week. Track:

- Are clicks growing month over month?
- Which blog posts get the most traffic?
- What new keywords are you ranking for?

Use this data to decide what to write next — double down on topics that already bring traffic.

---

## Conclusion

Google Search Console is one of the most valuable free tools for any blogger or developer with a website. It tells you:

1. How Google sees your site
2. What keywords bring you traffic
3. Which pages need improvement
4. How to get new content indexed fast

Set it up today, submit your sitemap, and check back weekly to grow your blog steadily.
