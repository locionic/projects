## Introduction

You've written great blog posts — but how do you know if anyone is actually finding them on Google? 

**Google Search Console (GSC)** is a free, essential tool from Google that shows you exactly how your site performs in search results, what keywords people search to find you, and what technical errors are holding you back.

<Callout type="info" title="The Setup">
  Head to **search.google.com/search-console**, add your domain, verify ownership via a DNS TXT record, and immediately submit your `sitemap.xml` so Google knows where your pages are!
</Callout>

---

## The Performance Report

The **Performance** tab is where the magic happens. It shows you your Total Clicks, Total Impressions, Average Click-Through Rate (CTR), and Average Position.

<Stepper>
  <Step title="Identify Low CTR Pages">
    Look for pages with high impressions but low clicks. This means Google is showing your page to users, but they aren't clicking it!
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optimize Meta Data">
    To fix a low CTR, rewrite your title and meta description. Make sure the title includes the target keyword and is compelling enough to grab attention!
  </Step>
  <Step title="Look for 'Striking Distance' Keywords">
    Find queries where your average position is 5–15. These are incredibly close to the top of Page 1. Slightly updating and expanding those blog posts can quickly push them into the top 3!
  </Step>
</Stepper>

---

## Handy GSC Tools

<Callout type="idea" title="URL Inspection Tool">
  Every time you publish a new blog post, paste its URL into the **URL Inspection** tool at the top of GSC and click "Request Indexing." This forces Google to crawl your new page immediately instead of waiting days!
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning" title="Watch the Coverage Report">
  Keep an eye on the **Coverage** tab. It will alert you to 404 errors (broken links), Soft 404s, or pages accidentally blocked by your `robots.txt` file.
</Callout>

Set a calendar reminder to review your Google Search Console data once a week. Double down on the topics that are already generating traffic, fix the errors, and watch your blog grow steadily!
