# IaaS, PaaS, SaaS: Who’s Responsible for What?

Cloud service tiers don't have to be alphabet soup. The fastest way to understand them is simple: **who manages the boring stuff?** It's all about how much of the tech stack you want to rent versus how much you want to control.

<Callout type="idea" title="The Golden Rule">
Pick based on how much operational responsibility you are willing to own!
</Callout>

---

## The Cloud Tiers Explained

<Stepper>
  <Step title="1. IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service) 🖥️">
    **You rent the computer.**
    You get raw computing power (servers, networking, storage). The provider handles the hardware, but *you* must manage the OS, middleware, and your application code.
    - **Best for:** Maximum control and custom infrastructure.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="2. PaaS (Platform as a Service) 🛠️">
    **You rent the place where apps run.**
    You skip the OS and server management. You bring your code, and the platform handles the scaling, runtimes, and plumbing behind the scenes.
    - **Best for:** Developers who want to deploy fast without herding servers.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="3. SaaS (Software as a Service) 🚀">
    **You rent the finished product.**
    You don’t provision servers or code. You just log into a browser and use the software (like Gmail, Salesforce, or Dropbox).
    - **Best for:** Consumers and businesses who just need tools that work immediately.
  </Step>
</Stepper>

---

<Callout type="info" title="Summary">
- **IaaS:** You manage the stack.
- **PaaS:** You manage the code.
- **SaaS:** You manage nothing but your data.
</Callout>
