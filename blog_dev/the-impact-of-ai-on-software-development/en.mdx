# The Impact of AI on Software Development: A Personal Reflection

As I come to terms with the reality that AI will likely write most of my code in the future, I'm left with a mix of emotions. On one hand, AI has already proven to be faster and more efficient in producing high-quality code, especially in languages and frameworks I'm less familiar with. On the other hand, I feel like I'm losing something valuable that I've spent years developing – my coding skills.

Learning to code was a daunting task, and it took a lot of effort to become proficient. I remember my first programming class in university, feeling lost in a complex codebase on my first job, and spending years practicing, learning from others, and reading books and blogs to improve my craft. The sense of accomplishment I felt when I finally mastered a new skill or solved a difficult problem was unparalleled.

Coding has always been a love-hate relationship for me. I loved the feeling of being "in the zone," balancing multiple ideas, and seeing my code come to life. But I also hated the focus and time required to write complex code, which often led to conflicts with time estimates.

Now, with AI taking over the coding process, I wonder if I'll still experience the same sense of satisfaction and accomplishment. Will I be able to find joy in instructing AI agents to write code, or will I feel like I'm losing my touch?

Perhaps the answer lies in shifting my focus to higher-level problems and using AI as a tool to augment my skills. By working with AI agents, I may be able to tackle more complex problems and find new ways to be creative and innovative.

Ultimately, the increasing use of AI in coding presents both opportunities and challenges for software engineers. While it may take away some of the personal satisfaction of writing code, it also offers the potential for greater efficiency, productivity, and growth.

As I navigate this new landscape, I'm reminded of the importance of adapting to change and finding new ways to add value in a rapidly evolving industry. By embracing AI and its potential, I hope to find new sources of satisfaction and fulfillment in my work as a software engineer.

### Key Takeaways

* AI is likely to play a significant role in coding in the future
* This shift may lead to a loss of personal satisfaction for software engineers
* However, AI also presents opportunities for greater efficiency, productivity, and growth
* Software engineers must adapt to change and find new ways to add value in a rapidly evolving industry
* Embracing AI and its potential can lead to new sources of satisfaction and fulfillment in work.
