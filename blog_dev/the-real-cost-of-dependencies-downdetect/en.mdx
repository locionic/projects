# The Real Cost of Dependencies: Downdetector's Experience with Cloudflare

During the November 2025 Cloudflare outage, Downdetector, a real-time outage and monitoring service, went down, exposing its reliance on Cloudflare. At first glance, this might seem counterintuitive, given Downdetector's purpose of monitoring uptime. However, a closer look reveals the complexities of designing a resilient system that balances cost, performance, and dependencies.

## Multi-Cloud Resilience and Dependencies

Downdetector was built with multi-region and multi-cloud architecture, which is essential for detecting cloud provider outages. However, this doesn't mean they can avoid dependencies altogether. In this case, Downdetector uses Cloudflare for DNS, Content Delivery (CDN), and Bot Protection. These services provide several benefits, including:

* Drastically lower bandwidth costs
* Faster load times due to edge nodes
* Protection from sudden traffic spikes and DDoS attacks
* Reduced infrastructure requirements

While it's possible for Downdetector to host everything on its own servers, the costs would surge, and the site would become slower to load. Given Downdetector's business model, which offers a free service to consumers, removing the Cloudflare dependency would not lead to increased revenue.

## The Pragmatic Choice

Dhruv Arora, Senior Director of Engineering at Ookla, the company behind Downdetector, confirmed that the decision to use Cloudflare was a pragmatic choice based on their business model. Building redundancy at the DNS and CDN layers would require significant overhead, and Cloudflare's Bot Protection is a world-class service that would be difficult to replicate.

## Lessons Learned

During the outage, the Downdetector team learned several valuable lessons:

* Having more Infrastructure as Code (IaC) could have helped bring the service back online sooner.
* The outage wasn't global, so they were able to shift traffic around and reduce the impact.
* Cloudflare's Bot Protection went haywire during the outage, blocking legitimate traffic, which required temporary disabling.

## Conclusion

Downdetector's experience highlights the trade-offs between cost, performance, and resilience when designing a system. While it's possible to build a system with no upstream dependencies, it's often not a pragmatic choice. By understanding the benefits and limitations of dependencies, teams can make informed decisions about their architecture and design.
