# The Evolution of Software Development: Insights from Thoughtworks' Technology Radar

Thoughtworks has released the 34th volume of its Technology Radar, a biannual survey of the technology scene. This edition focuses on tools, techniques, platforms, and languages that have caught the attention of the Thoughtworks team. With 118 blips, the radar provides a comprehensive overview of the current state of software development.

## The Rise of AI-Oriented Topics

The radar is dominated by AI-oriented topics, reflecting the significant impact of artificial intelligence on software development. This includes the use of Large Language Models (LLMs) in software development, which is forcing developers to revisit the foundations of their craft. Established techniques such as pair programming, zero-trust architecture, and mutation testing are being reevaluated in the context of AI-assisted development.

## The Resurgence of the Command Line

The radar also notes a resurgence of the command line as a primary interface for developers. After years of abstracting away the command line in favor of usability, agentic tools are bringing developers back to the terminal. This shift is driven by the need for more control and flexibility in software development.

## Securing "Permission Hungry" Agents

One of the themes of the radar is securing "permission hungry" agents, which require broad access to private data, external communication, and real systems. This creates a bind between the need for access and the risk of security breaches. The radar includes several blips suggesting guides and sensors necessary for a well-fitting harness to secure these agents.

## The Importance of Human Review in AI-Assisted Development

Mike Mason's article on the dangers of not reading code generated by LLMs highlights the importance of human review in AI-assisted development. While LLMs can generate working code, they can also produce incomprehensible messes. Human review is necessary to ensure that the code is maintainable, durable, and secure.

## The Ethics of LLMs in Software Development

Dan Davies poses a thought-provoking philosophy question about the ethics of LLMs indulging in ghost writing. This raises important questions about the role of LLMs in software development and the need for transparency and accountability.

## The Importance of Public Service in Government Technology Initiatives

Don Moynihan's essay on the demise of DirectFile, a government program that supported people filing their taxes online, highlights the importance of public service in government technology initiatives. The essay notes that the simpler a potential change appears, the more likely it is to have deceptive complexity that others have tried and failed to resolve.

In conclusion, the 34th Technology Radar provides valuable insights into the current state of software development, emphasizing the importance of security, human review, and public service in the age of AI-assisted development. As the technology landscape continues to evolve, it is essential to prioritize these principles to ensure that software development serves the greater good.

**Key Takeaways:**

* AI-oriented topics dominate the 34th Technology Radar
* Established techniques are being reevaluated in the context of AI-assisted development
* The command line is experiencing a resurgence as a primary interface for developers
* Securing "permission hungry" agents is a critical theme in the radar
* Human review is necessary to ensure that code generated by LLMs is maintainable, durable, and secure
* Public service is essential in government technology initiatives

**Recommendations:**

* Prioritize security in AI-assisted development
* Emphasize human review in AI-assisted development
* Foster a culture of public service in government technology initiatives
* Continuously monitor and evaluate the impact of AI on software development
