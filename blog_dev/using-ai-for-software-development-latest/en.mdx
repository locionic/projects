# Using AI for Software Development: Latest Insights and Developments

The field of software development is rapidly evolving with the increasing use of Artificial Intelligence (AI). In this post, we will discuss some of the latest developments and insights in using AI for software development.

## Chris Parsons' Updated Guide on Using AI to Code

Chris Parsons has recently updated his guide on using AI to code, which provides a wealth of information on how to effectively use AI in software development. The guide emphasizes the importance of keeping changes small, building guardrails, documenting ruthlessly, and verifying every change before it ships. Parsons also highlights the distinction between "vibe coding" and "agentic engineering," and recommends using tools like Claude Code or Codex CLI.

## Harness Engineering: A Key Aspect of Agentic Programming

Birgitta Böckeler has written a superb article on Harness Engineering, which is a crucial aspect of agentic programming. Böckeler's work focuses on the role of computational sensors in the harness, such as static analysis and tests. She argues that Large Language Models (LLMs) are great for exploratory and fuzzy rules, but once you have something that is objective, converting it to a formal, unambiguous, deterministic format can give you more assurance.

## Function Length in Agentic Programming

Adam Tornhill has considered the age-old question of how long a function should be in the context of agentic programming. Tornhill argues that the answer is not to think about how many lines should be in a function, but rather to focus on providing better structure. He suggests that functions should define useful concepts and wrap code in a way that communicates intent and localizes change.

## The Importance of Precise and Consistent Definitions

Nilay Patel's post on "Why People Hate AI" highlights the importance of precise and consistent definitions in software development. Patel argues that the "Software Brain" view of the world, which sees everything as a series of databases that can be controlled with software code, can be alienating and uncomfortable for many people. He suggests that this view is problematic because it reduces people to data points and ignores the complexities of human experience.

## The Role of Conceptual Modeling in Agentic Programming

The importance of precise and consistent definitions is also relevant to the use of LLMs in software development. Conceptual modeling is a key skill for agentic programming, as it allows developers to communicate effectively with LLMs and to define the concepts and relationships that underlie their code.

## The Future of AI in Software Development

Ezra Klein's post on "The New Feeling in San Francisco" highlights the sense of insecurity and competition that is driving the adoption of AI in the tech industry. Klein argues that the advantages of working with AI will compound over time, and that those who are able to effectively integrate AI into their lives and companies will be at a significant advantage. However, this also raises concerns about the legibility of human experience to AI systems, and the potential for AI to reduce human complexity to data points.

## Conclusion

In conclusion, the use of AI in software development is a rapidly evolving field that is raising important questions about the nature of code, the role of human experience, and the future of work. By understanding the latest developments and insights in this field, developers can better navigate the challenges and opportunities of agentic programming and harness the power of AI to create more effective and efficient software systems.
