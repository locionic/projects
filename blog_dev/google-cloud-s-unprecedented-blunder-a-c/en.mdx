# Google Cloud's Unprecedented Blunder: A Cautionary Tale for Cloud Users

A recent incident involving Google Cloud and a major Australian trading fund, UniSuper, serves as a stark reminder of the importance of having a backup plan in place when using cloud services. On April 29, Google Cloud accidentally deleted UniSuper's subscription, which also deleted all associated data. Fortunately, UniSuper had set up a backup on another service provider outside of Google Cloud, which prevented data loss.

## The Incident

UniSuper, one of the largest retirement savings accounts in Australia, had $124B of assets under management and relied on Google Cloud for its infrastructure. Despite setting up replication across two regions in Google Cloud to protect against regional failures, the cloud provider deleted the replica as well. The incident resulted in a two-week outage, during which members could not log into their online accounts or manage their funds.

## A Rare Admission of Fault

In a surprising move, Google Cloud's CEO, Thomas Kurian, issued a joint statement with UniSuper, taking full responsibility for the failure. This admission of fault is rare in the industry, and it highlights the severity of the incident.

## The Importance of Backup and Disaster Recovery

The incident serves as a reminder that even the largest and most reliable cloud providers can make mistakes. It is essential to have a backup plan in place to prevent data loss in the event of a disaster. UniSuper's decision to invest in a third-party backup proved to be a wise one, as it allowed them to restore services quickly.

## Lessons Learned

The incident highlights several key takeaways:

* **Don't put all your eggs in one basket**: Having a single point of failure can be catastrophic. Consider using multiple cloud providers or on-premises backups to ensure business continuity.
* **Backup and disaster recovery are crucial**: Invest in a robust backup and disaster recovery strategy to prevent data loss in the event of a disaster.
* **Cloud providers are not infallible**: Even the largest and most reliable cloud providers can make mistakes. Don't assume that your data is safe just because it's in the cloud.

## Conclusion

The incident involving Google Cloud and UniSuper serves as a cautionary tale for cloud users. It highlights the importance of having a backup plan in place and not relying solely on a single cloud provider. By investing in a robust backup and disaster recovery strategy, businesses can ensure business continuity and prevent data loss in the event of a disaster.
