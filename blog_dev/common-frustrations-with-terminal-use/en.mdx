# Common Frustrations with Terminal Use

A recent survey of 1600 terminal users aimed to identify the most common frustrations people experience when using the terminal. The survey, which was conducted over a couple of days on Mastodon and Twitter, collected responses from users with varying levels of experience, including 40% who have been using the terminal for 21+ years and 95% who have been using it for at least 4 years.

## Top Categories of Frustrations

After categorizing the responses, the following top categories of frustrations emerged:

### 1. Remembering Syntax (115)

Users struggle to remember syntax for various CLI tools, such as `awk`, `jq`, `sed`, and keyboard shortcuts for `tmux` and text editing. One user commented, "There are just so many little 'trivia' details to remember for full functionality."

### 2. Switching Terminals is Hard (91)

Users experience difficulties when switching between systems, such as differences in keyboard shortcuts, text editors, and command versions. For example, "I got used to fish and vi mode which are not available when I ssh into servers, containers."

### 3. Color Scheme Issues (85)

Users encounter problems with color schemes, including unreadable colors, difficulty finding a suitable color scheme, and inconsistencies across different apps. One user shared, "Getting my terminal theme configured in a reasonable way between the terminal emulator and fish... was tedious and fiddly."

### 4. Keyboard Shortcuts (84)

Half of the comments on keyboard shortcuts mentioned the difference in copy/paste shortcuts between Linux/Windows and the rest of the OS. Other issues include limited keyboard shortcut support and conflicts with OS shortcuts.

### 5. Copy and Paste Issues (75)

Aside from keyboard shortcut differences, users experience problems with copying and pasting, such as dealing with multiple clipboards, synchronizing them, and random spaces added when copying from the terminal.

### 6. Discoverability (55)

Users struggle to discover useful tools or features, with one comment summarizing the issue: "How difficult it is to learn independently. Most of what I know is an assorted collection of stuff I've been told by random people over the years."

### 7. Steep Learning Curve (44)

Users find the terminal has a steep learning curve, with comments like, "After 15 years of using it, I'm not much faster than using it than I was 5 or maybe even 10 years ago."

### 8. History (42)

Users experience issues with shell history, including history not being shared between terminal tabs, limits being too short, and history not being restored when terminal tabs are restored.

### 9. Bad Documentation (37)

Users complain about opaque documentation, lack of examples in man pages, and programs without man pages. One comment represents the frustration: "Finding good examples and docs. Man pages often not enough, have to wade through stack overflow."

### 10. Scrollback (36)

Users encounter issues with scrollback, including programs printing too much data, resizing the terminal messing up scrollback, and lack of timestamps.

## Other Notable Frustrations

Other frustrations mentioned at least 10 times include:

* Inconsistent command line arguments
* Keeping dotfiles in sync across different systems
* Performance issues
* Window management
* Feeling scared or uneasy when using the terminal
* Terminfo issues
* Lack of image support
* SSH issues
* Tmux/screen issues
* Typos and slow typing
* Terminal getting messed up for various reasons
* Quoting/escaping in the shell

These categories and issues highlight the complexities and challenges of using the terminal, even for experienced users.
