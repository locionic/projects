# Migrating to HTTP/3 with Prometheus: A Journey of Scalable Network Probing

## The Challenge of Legacy Tooling

Slack's network measurement approach, which combines internal and external solutions, faced a significant challenge with the rollout of HTTP/3 support. The lack of client-side observability due to HTTP/3's use of the QUIC transport protocol and UDP instead of TCP, rendered existing monitoring tools and SaaS solutions ineffective. The market gap in supporting HTTP/3 probing out of the box, and the absence of native QUIC support in Prometheus Blackbox Exporter, necessitated a custom solution.

## Open-Sourcing QUIC Support for Prometheus Blackbox Exporter

Sebastian Feliciano, an intern at Slack, took on the challenge of implementing and open-sourcing QUIC support for Prometheus Blackbox Exporter. The journey involved:

### Choosing the Right HTTP Client

After careful consideration, quic-go was chosen as the foundation for the new functionality due to its wide adoption and first-class support for creating HTTP clients in Go.

```go
http3Transport := &http3.Transport{
    TLSClientConfig: tlsConfig,
    QUICConfig: &quic.Config{},
}
client = &http.Client{
    Transport: http3Transport,
}
```

### Maintaining Composability

The new logic was added while following the existing architecture of Blackbox Exporter, ensuring the new features maintained the tool's configuration patterns.

## Integration and Operational Improvements

The open-sourced contribution provided a solution for the entire Prometheus community. The integration of the new feature enabled:

* A unified view of HTTP/1.1, HTTP/2, and HTTP/3 metrics in Grafana
* Better and more reliable alerts on the health and performance of HTTP/3 endpoints
* Easier correlation of HTTP/3 performance with other metrics and debugging of issues

## The Open Source Win

The contribution benefits the wider Prometheus community, helping organizations facing similar challenges with HTTP/3 adoption. The support for QUIC and HTTP/3 has future-proofed observability for ongoing adoption.

## Future Improvements

Potential future improvements include:

* Server Name Indication (SNI) routing tests
* End-to-end path visualization

## Conclusion

The project highlights the importance of:

1. **Monitor first, and migrate second**: Observability is crucial before migration.
2. **Contributing open source pays dividends**: Giving back to open source communities benefits everyone.
3. **Bet on your interns**: Encouraging intern contributions can lead to significant innovations.

Slack's commitment to simplicity and scalability is evident in this journey. As HTTP/3 adoption grows, the company is dedicated to keeping monitoring tools ahead of the curve.
