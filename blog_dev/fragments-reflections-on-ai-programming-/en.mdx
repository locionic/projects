# Fragments: Reflections on AI, Programming, and Society

## On LLM-Augmented Programming

Kent Beck and I recently discussed our experiences with LLM-augmented programming at the GOTO Conference in Copenhagen. We shared our frustrations about the need for a new generation to lead the charge in software development and the importance of junior developers focusing on their craft.

Ian Johnson's series on restructuring a gnarly codebase offers valuable insights into the process. He highlights the importance of characterization tests, static analysis, and architectural patterns in enabling effective use of AI agents. By leveraging these tools, he was able to shift from writing code to curating the output of the AI agent.

## The Challenges of Working with AI

Adam Tornhill notes that working with AI agents can be mentally expensive. The increased decision density and need for constant verification can be taxing. He advocates for breaking tasks into smaller, manageable chunks and automating as much as possible.

## The Limitations of Human Attention

Tornhill also emphasizes the importance of focusing on a single task at a time. The idea of running multiple AI agents in parallel may be appealing, but it can lead to cognitive overload. Humans are not equipped to handle the parallelization of attention, and it's essential to prioritize a more focused approach.

## The Need for Regulation

The US Government's latest efforts to regulate AI have been met with skepticism. The proposed regulations are seen as too mild, and the postponement of the executive order has raised concerns about the government's role in shaping AI policy.

Lawfare argues that the lack of governmental expertise in AI and software development is a significant problem. The tech oligarchy's influence on policy decisions is concerning, and it's essential to engage in broader discussions about the impact of AI on society.

## A Call for Expertise in Politics

The need for expertise in software development and AI is not limited to the tech industry. It's essential to have knowledgeable individuals in politics who can make informed decisions about AI regulation. Beth Anders-Beck, a candidate for congressional district MA-06, is an example of someone with a strong background in software development who could bring valuable expertise to Congress.

## The Future of Work and Society

The Economist notes that historical technological advances have not led to significant unemployment or drops in wages. However, the impact of AI on graduate hiring is already being felt, with employment rates dropping in fields with high exposure to AI.

As we navigate the challenges and opportunities presented by AI, it's essential to consider the broader social implications. The need for regulation, expertise, and informed decision-making is more pressing than ever.
