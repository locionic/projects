# The Decline of the FDE Role: What's Behind the Lack of Interest?

As the tech industry continues to evolve, new trends and insights emerge that challenge our understanding of the job market. A recent article in The Wall Street Journal highlights an interesting phenomenon: despite a surge in job postings for Full-Stack Developer Engineer (FDE) roles, few engineers are interested in taking on the position. In this article, we'll explore the reasons behind this trend and what it means for the future of software engineering.

## The Rise and Fall of the FDE Role

Last summer, the FDE role was considered a highly sought-after position, with many engineers eager to take on the challenge. However, after speaking with several engineers who have taken on FDE roles, it's clear that the reality of the job is far from the initial expectations. The role seems to have evolved into a "sales engineer" or "solutions engineer" position, where FDEs focus on integrating software and deploying solutions for customers, rather than contributing to the platform or engaging in software engineering.

## The Reality of the FDE Role

Two engineers who took on FDE roles shared their experiences, revealing a stark contrast between the initial expectations and the reality of the job. One engineer described the role as having a "typical IT services mindset," with a focus on engagement lead and little to no software development. This mismatch between expectations and reality has led to disappointment and frustration among engineers, with some even quitting their jobs shortly after starting.

## Why the Lack of Interest?

So, why are engineers no longer interested in taking on FDE roles? The answer lies in the nature of the job itself. The FDE role has become less about software engineering and more about sales and deployment. This shift in focus has made the role less desirable for engineers who are passionate about building software and contributing to the platform.

## What's Next for the FDE Role?

As the tech industry continues to evolve, it's clear that the FDE role will need to adapt to meet the changing needs of engineers and companies alike. Whether this means redefining the role to focus more on software engineering or finding new ways to make the position more desirable, one thing is certain: the FDE role will need to change if it's to remain a viable career path for engineers.
