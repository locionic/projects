# How to Harden Firefox on Linux 

Firefox is already a far better starting point for privacy than Chrome or Edge. It's open source and isn't built by an ad company. However, out of the box, it still phones home and leaves many tracking protections off.

Let's fix that—without going down a three-hour rabbit hole.

<Callout type="danger" title="Before You Start: Backup Your Profile!">
  Most guides skip this! Close Firefox and run: `cp -r ~/.mozilla/firefox/ ~/firefox-backup/`. If anything breaks, you can restore your entire browser in seconds.
</Callout>

## Essential Hardening Steps

<Stepper>
  <Step title="Step 1: Turn Off Telemetry & AI">
    Go to **Settings → Privacy & Security**. Uncheck everything under "Firefox Data Collection and Use." Also, head to **AI Controls** and turn off AI enhancements to stop data routing to third parties.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Step 2: Switch the Default Search">
    Go to **Settings → Search**. Swap Google out for **DuckDuckGo**, **Startpage**, or **Brave Search**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Step 3: Tighten Tracking Protection">
    Under **Privacy & Security**, change Enhanced Tracking Protection to **Custom**. Block all cross-site cookies and suspected fingerprinters/cryptominers in all windows.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Step 4: Enable HTTPS-Only & Secure DNS">
    Scroll to the bottom of Privacy settings. Enable **HTTPS-Only Mode** in all windows. Then, set **DNS over HTTPS** to "Max Protection" (Cloudflare or Quad9 are great choices).
  </Step>
</Stepper>

## Level Up: `about:config` Tweaks

<Callout type="info" title="Don't Be Afraid">
  Type `about:config` in the address bar. Accept the risk, and safely modify these values:
  - `privacy.resistFingerprinting = true` (Makes your browser look generic)
  - `privacy.partition.network_state = true` (Total Cookie Protection)
  - `extensions.pocket.enabled = false` (Disables Pocket)
</Callout>

## The Right Extensions

You don't need 10 privacy extensions. More extensions = a more unique fingerprint! Just install these:

1. **uBlock Origin:** The gold standard ad/tracker blocker. Enable the extra privacy/security filter lists in its dashboard.
2. **Firefox Multi-Account Containers:** Isolate your browsing (e.g., Google stuff in one container, personal in another).
3. **Bitwarden:** Because the most private browser won't save you if you reuse weak passwords.

<Callout type="idea" title="Pro Tip: Use Multiple Profiles">
  Type `about:profiles` and create a dedicated profile for sensitive browsing (banking, healthcare) and keep another profile relaxed for streaming media. Launch them simultaneously with `firefox -P "ProfileName" --no-remote`.
</Callout>
