# Side Hustles vs. Passive Income

If you want to build financial freedom, you need to understand the difference between hustling for money and making your money hustle for you.

<Callout type="idea" title="The Core Difference">
**Side Hustles** = Trading your time and effort for cash (Active Income).
**Passive Income** = Building assets or systems that pay you while you sleep.
</Callout>

---

## 1. Active Side Hustles

Side hustles are great for generating immediate cash flow to pay off debt or fund investments. But remember: when you stop working, the money stops.

<Stepper>
  <Step title="Freelancing">Sell your skills (writing, coding, design) on Upwork or Fiverr.</Step>
  <Step title="Gig Economy">Drive for DoorDash, Uber, or do tasks on TaskRabbit.</Step>
  <Step title="Selling & Teaching">Resell items on eBay, run an Etsy store, or tutor online.</Step>
</Stepper>

## 2. Passive Income Streams

Passive income requires heavy upfront work or capital, but it scales endlessly and frees up your time.

<Stepper>
  <Step title="Digital Products">Create an e-book, templates, or online courses. Build once, sell infinitely.</Step>
  <Step title="Investing">Stocks, dividend portfolios, or high-yield savings accounts.</Step>
  <Step title="Real Estate & Royalties">Rental properties (or REITs), and licensing music, art, or patents.</Step>
</Stepper>

---

## The Action Plan

<Callout type="info" title="How to Start">
1. **Assess Resources:** Figure out your available time, skills, and capital.
2. **Start Active:** Launch a side hustle for quick wins.
3. **Transition to Passive:** Reinvest your side hustle profits into passive assets (stocks, real estate, digital products).
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning" title="Reality Check">
There is no such thing as "100% passive" income from day one. Everything requires upfront effort. Start small today, and your future self will thank you.
</Callout>
