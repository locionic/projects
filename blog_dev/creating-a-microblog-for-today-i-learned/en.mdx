# Creating a Microblog for Today I Learned (TIL) Notes

As a way to save and share interesting tools and facts, I recently added a new section to my site called TIL (Today I Learned). The idea behind this microblog is to have a dedicated space for storing useful information that I can easily access and share with others.

### The Problem: Saving Useful Information

When I come across a cool tool or fact, I often want to share it with others, but I don't necessarily want to write a full blog post about it. I've found that social media platforms like Mastodon and Bluesky are great for sharing quick tips and resources, but they can be ephemeral, and it's easy to lose track of the information.

### The Solution: Creating a TIL Microblog

To solve this problem, I created a new folder on my site called `/til/` and set up a separate RSS feed for it. I also created a custom Rake task to help me quickly create new posts, and I styled the posts to look a bit like tweets.

### Inspiration from Simon Willison's TIL Blog

My TIL microblog was inspired by Simon Willison's great TIL blog, although my posts are typically much shorter. I like the idea of having a dedicated space for storing useful information, and I find that it's easier to keep track of things when they're in a public space.

### The Benefits of a TIL Microblog

Having a TIL microblog has been working well for me so far. I can quickly create new posts in just a few minutes, and it's been helpful to have a dedicated space for storing useful information. I also appreciate that it's easy to share my TIL posts with others via the RSS feed.

### The Philosophy Behind My TIL Microblog

I've been thinking a lot about what I want to do with my online presence, and I've come to realize that I don't need to archive everything. Some things are meant to be ephemeral, and that's okay. I like the idea of having a mix of permanent and ephemeral content online. My TIL microblog is one way that I'm implementing this philosophy, and I'm excited to see how it evolves over time.

### Next Steps

I'm considering adding a quick summary of my TIL posts to my weekly mailing list, so that subscribers can stay up-to-date with my latest discoveries. I'm also excited to continue using my TIL microblog as a way to share useful information with others.
