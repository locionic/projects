# Creating a Microblog for Today I Learned (TIL) Notes

As a way to save and share interesting tools and facts, I recently added a new section to my site called **TIL (Today I Learned)**. The idea behind this microblog is to have a dedicated space for storing useful information that I can easily access and share with others.

<Callout type="warning" title="The Ephemeral Web Problem">
  When I come across a cool tool or fact, I often share it on social platforms like Mastodon or Bluesky. While great for quick tips, this content is ephemeral—it gets lost in the feed instantly!
</Callout>

### The Solution: A TIL Microblog

To solve this, I created a new `/til/` folder on my site and styled the posts to look a bit like tweets!

<Stepper>
  <Step title="Dedicated RSS Feed">
    I set up a separate RSS feed just for this folder so people can subscribe to my quick tips independently of my main blog.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Custom Rake Task">
    I created a custom Rake task in my project to instantly generate new post templates, reducing friction when I want to quickly jot something down.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Keep it Brief">
    Inspired by Simon Willison's TIL blog, my posts are bite-sized, making it incredibly easy to document and share findings publicly.
  </Step>
</Stepper>

<Callout type="idea" title="My New Philosophy">
  I've come to realize I don't need to archive everything into massive, heavily-edited articles. Having a mix of permanent long-form content and ephemeral, quick-fire notes creates a much healthier online presence.
</Callout>

### Next Steps

The TIL microblog has been a fantastic success so far! Up next, I'm considering adding a quick summary of my TIL posts to my weekly mailing list so subscribers can stay up-to-date with my latest discoveries.
