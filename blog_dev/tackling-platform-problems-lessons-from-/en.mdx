# Tackling Platform Problems: Lessons from GitHub Engineers

As a software engineer, I've found that building complex systems is similar to building intricate model kits, like those found in the Gundam universe. Product engineers focus on building the final product, while platform engineers provide the tools and infrastructure necessary to support the product. In this post, I'll share my team's experience transitioning from product engineering to platform engineering at GitHub and the best practices we've learned along the way.

## Understanding Your Domain

Before tackling platform problems, it's essential to understand the domain. This requires gaining knowledge of technical terms, system interactions, and the business context. Here are some steps to get up to speed:

* **Talk to your neighbors**: Arrange a handover meeting with a team that has more knowledge and experience with the subject matter.
* **Investigate old issues**: Review stale or persistent issues to gain a deeper understanding of the system's limitations and potential areas for improvement.
* **Read the docs**: Documentation is a valuable resource that can help you understand how the system works.

## Bridging Concepts to Platform-Specific Skills

While the above advice is applicable to both product and platform teams, platform teams require a more in-depth understanding of specific concepts, including:

* **Networks**: Understand network fundamentals, such as TCP, UDP, and L4 load balancing, as well as debugging tools like dig.
* **Operating systems and hardware**: Select appropriate virtual machines (VMs) or physical hardware for scalability and cost management.
* **Infrastructure as Code (IaC)**: Proficiency in tools like Terraform, Ansible, and Consul is essential for automating infrastructure provisioning and modifications.
* **Distributed systems**: Understand that failures are inevitable and employ proactive solutions like failover and recovery mechanisms to preserve system reliability.

## Knowledge Sharing

Sharing lessons and ideas is crucial for introducing new perspectives, leading to breakthroughs and innovations. Here are three reasons why knowledge sharing is essential:

* **Teamwork makes the dream work**: Collaboration leads to quicker problem resolution and fosters new solution innovation.
* **Prevent lost knowledge**: Sharing lessons learned prevents information from being lost when an engineer leaves the company or is unavailable.
* **Improve customer success**: By sharing knowledge, engineers can build reliable, scalable, and secure platforms that meet customer needs and expectations.

## Impact Radius

Platform changes can have a significant impact on downstream dependencies. To understand the impact radius:

* **Direct communication**: Communicate with teams that depend on your service to gain valuable insights into how proposed changes may affect other services.
* **Postmortems**: Analyze past incidents to understand the impact of changes or failures and how they were fixed.
* **Monitoring and telemetry**: Use tools like Single Availability Metrics (SAM) to quickly assess the system's health and pinpoint issues.

## Testing Changes

Testing changes in a distributed environment can be challenging. Here are some strategies to overcome this:

* **Test site**: Use a test site as a "real" machine to implement and assess changes.
* **Infrastructure as Code (IaC)**: Test fundamental operations like provisioning and deprovisioning machines.
* **End-to-End (E2E)**: Direct some network traffic to servers and observe host behavior or evaluate functionality by diverting a small portion of traffic.
* **Self-healing**: Test the platform's ability to recover from unexpected loads and identify bottlenecks before they impact users.

## Conclusion

Tackling platform problems can be challenging, but with the right approach, it can be rewarding. By understanding the domain, bridging concepts to platform-specific skills, knowledge sharing, and testing changes in a distributed environment, engineers can build reliable, scalable, and secure platforms that meet customer needs and expectations.
