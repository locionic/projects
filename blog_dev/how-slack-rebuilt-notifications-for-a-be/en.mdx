# How Slack Rebuilt Notifications for a Better User Experience

At Slack, notifications play a crucial role in keeping teams informed, but they can also become overwhelming if not designed thoughtfully. The goal of rebuilding Slack's notification system was to bring calm, consistency, and clarity to the experience, making it effortless for users to stay informed.

## Diagnosing the Noise Problem

Research revealed that notification overload was a universal challenge affecting teams across various workspaces. The more channels a person joined, the more likely they were to feel overwhelmed and confused about notification behavior. Internally, data showed that issues with notifications were among the top drivers of Customer Experience tickets, with users often unsure how to control or understand their settings.

The noise problem wasn't just about volume; it was deeply rooted in the architecture of the legacy notification system. Over time, the system had accumulated complexity, making it difficult for users to understand or control. Four key issues contributed to the noise:

* **Conflicting mental models**: Desktop and mobile had different preference systems, with distinct options and behaviors.
* **Hidden coupling between preferences**: What users were notified about was tightly coupled with how they received notifications.
* **Inconsistent state across clients**: Settings didn't reliably sync, leading to confusion and duplicate configuration work.
* **Power users left behind**: Advanced controls were scattered across multiple menus with no clear hierarchy.

These architectural problems directly contributed to the noise users experienced, making it essential to rebuild the notification system from the ground up.

## Simplifying Notifications at Scale

The project aimed to comprehensively address user pain points and confusion by introducing the following improvements:

* **Simpler choices**: Channel notifications now have three clear options: All new posts, Mentions, or Mute.
* **Push toggles**: Unified on/off options for push notifications across desktop and mobile.
* **Advanced controls**: Redesigned settings for power users, including "badge all unreads" on mobile.
* **Global preferences**: Modernized desktop and mobile experiences with consistent structure and copy.
* **Sync improvements**: Consistent state across clients through simplified preference logic.

## Technical Challenges and Solutions

The project tackled three major technical challenges:

1. **The Preference Refactor**: Migrating millions of users from four conflicting preference systems to one unified model.
2. **Modal Makeover and Global Preferences**: Separating "what" from "how" with auto-save behavior and consistent cross-platform UI.
3. **Cross-Platform Parity**: Achieving true state consistency between mobile and desktop.

To overcome these challenges, the team employed various technical strategies, including:

* **Read-time fallbacks**: Ensuring users had the same experience as before, even during rollbacks.
* **Decoupling push logic**: Separating push notifications from activity and in-app notifications.
* **Renaming fields and refactoring client logic**: Eliminating ambiguity and making rollbacks safe.
* **Rewriting oldest pages in Slack's iOS app**: Ensuring consistency with desktop structure and visuals.

## Migration and Rollback Lessons

Migrating millions of users without disruption required careful mapping and fallbacks. Key lessons learned include:

* **Trust must never break**: Adding read-time fallbacks to ensure users had the same experience as before, even during rollbacks.
* **Clarity beats cleverness**: Removing the sync parameter and storing explicit desktop and mobile values made behavior predictable.

## Closing Reflection

The project was a rebuild of trust, aiming to make staying informed feel effortless. The unified model means users now control noise with confidence, and the architecture matches users' mental models, making behavior predictable. The data proves it: user engagement increased, and support burden decreased significantly.

## What Made it Work

* **Close collaboration**: Across design, frontend, backend, and mobile teams.
* **Courage to revisit legacy systems**: Instead of patching them.
* **Shared alignment on clarity over speed**: Collective ownership across the Messaging pillar.

The project demonstrated that deep technical simplification can create emotional calm for millions of users. When the system matches how people think, staying informed becomes effortless, and that's when Slack becomes the calm, focused workspace teams deserve.
