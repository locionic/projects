# The Rise of Forward Deployed Engineers: What's Behind the Growing Demand?

As we continue to navigate the rapidly evolving tech landscape, one trend is becoming increasingly clear: the demand for forward deployed engineers (FDEs) is on the rise. In this post, we'll explore the reasons behind this growing demand and what it means for the industry.

## Google Doubles Down on FDEs

Google is leading the charge in FDE recruitment, with a significant spike in hiring activity. The company's Cloud CEO, Thomas Kurian, has announced a new AI-focused organization within the Go-To-Market team, and is actively hiring FDEs to support this initiative. The hiring process has been streamlined, with some candidates reportedly going through as few as two interviews in just two days. This suggests that Google is eager to fill these roles quickly and is willing to adapt its hiring process to achieve this goal.

## OpenAI and Anthropic Follow Suit

OpenAI and Anthropic are also investing heavily in FDEs. OpenAI has announced the creation of a standalone entity, The OpenAI Deployment Company, which will focus on deploying AI solutions to customers. This company will be responsible for hiring FDEs to work closely with business leaders, operators, and frontline teams to identify areas where AI can make a significant impact. Anthropic is also creating a distinct FDE consulting company, which will work with mid-sized companies to bring its AI model, Claude, into their operations.

## The Evolving Role of FDEs

As the demand for FDEs grows, the role itself is evolving. Initially, FDEs were seen as a mix of platform engineering, software engineering, and solutions engineering. However, with the creation of standalone companies and external funding, the role is becoming more similar to that of a solutions architect or consultant. FDEs are now expected to work closely with customers, integrating AI solutions into their workflows and providing "white glove" deployment services.

## The Reality of FDE Roles

While job adverts for FDEs may sound impressive, the reality of the role is often more nuanced. FDEs are expected to code, integrate, and debug AI solutions, but they also spend a significant amount of time on meetings and customer hand-holding. The role requires a unique blend of technical skills, business acumen, and problem-solving abilities.

## Opportunities for New Grads

The growing demand for FDEs presents an opportunity for new graduates entering the industry. Many tech companies, including product companies, consulting firms, and IT services companies, will be hiring FDEs to support their AI adoption efforts. This role can provide new grads with valuable experience, real-world learning opportunities, and a chance to work with cutting-edge technologies.

## Industry-Wide Demand

The demand for FDEs is not limited to Google, OpenAI, and Anthropic. Many companies, including AI labs, AI vendors, non-AI companies, and SaaS companies, will be looking to hire FDEs to support their AI adoption efforts. This trend is expected to continue, with FDEs playing a critical role in speeding up AI rollouts and driving revenue growth.

## Conclusion

The growing demand for FDEs is a significant trend in the tech industry, driven by the need for companies to adopt and deploy AI solutions quickly and effectively. As the role of FDEs evolves, it presents opportunities for new graduates and experienced professionals alike. Whether you're a tech company looking to hire FDEs or an individual looking to pursue a career in this field, understanding the growing demand for FDEs is essential for success in the rapidly evolving tech landscape.
