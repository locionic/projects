# Encoding Team Standards for AI Interactions

Artificial Intelligence (AI) coding assistants have become increasingly popular in software development. However, the quality of the code generated by these assistants depends on the quality of the prompts provided to them. To ensure consistency in the quality of the generated code, it is essential to articulate and encode team standards.

## The Problem with Tacit Knowledge

Tacit knowledge refers to the unwritten rules, conventions, and best practices that are shared among team members. However, this knowledge is often not explicitly documented and is instead passed down through oral tradition or learned through experience. This can lead to inconsistencies in the code generated by AI assistants, as they may not have access to this tacit knowledge.

## Treating AI Instructions as Infrastructure

Rahul Garg proposes treating the instructions that govern AI interactions as infrastructure. This means versioning, reviewing, and sharing these instructions as artifacts that encode tacit team knowledge into executable instructions. By doing so, teams can ensure that the quality of the generated code is consistent, regardless of who is interacting with the AI assistant.

## Benefits of Encoding Team Standards

Encoding team standards has several benefits, including:

* **Consistent Quality**: By encoding team standards, teams can ensure that the generated code meets the same quality standards, regardless of who is interacting with the AI assistant.
* **Improved Productivity**: With encoded team standards, teams can reduce the time spent on reviewing and refactoring code generated by AI assistants.
* **Increased Transparency**: Encoding team standards provides a clear understanding of the rules and conventions that govern the code generation process.

## Implementing Encoded Team Standards

To implement encoded team standards, teams can follow these steps:

* **Identify Tacit Knowledge**: Identify the unwritten rules, conventions, and best practices that are shared among team members.
* **Document Standards**: Document these standards in a clear and concise manner.
* **Version and Review**: Version and review these standards to ensure that they are accurate and up-to-date.
* **Share Artifacts**: Share these artifacts with the AI assistant to ensure that it generates code that meets the team's standards.

By encoding team standards, teams can ensure that the quality of the generated code is consistent, and improve productivity and transparency in the code generation process.
