# Code Orange: Enhancing Cloudflare's Network Resilience and Security

Cloudflare has recently completed an intensive engineering effort, code-named "Code Orange: Fail Small," aimed at improving the resilience, security, and reliability of its infrastructure. This initiative was a response to the global outages that occurred on November 18, 2025, and December 5, 2025, and its completion marks a significant milestone in Cloudflare's ongoing efforts to ensure the highest level of service quality for its customers.

## Safer Configuration Changes

To minimize the risk of configuration-related outages, Cloudflare has implemented a new approach to configuration changes. Instead of deploying changes instantly across the network, the company now uses a "health-mediated deployment" methodology, which involves gradual rollout, real-time health monitoring, and automated rollback. This approach is made possible by a new internal component called Snapstone, which bundles configuration changes into packages and allows for progressive release with health mediation.

Snapstone provides a unified way to bring progressive rollout, real-time health monitoring, and automated rollback to configuration deployments by default. Its flexibility enables teams to dynamically define any unit of configuration that needs health mediation, whether it's a data file or a control flag in the global configuration system. This gives Cloudflare the ability to quickly identify and fix potential issues before they affect customer traffic.

## Reducing the Impact of Failure

In the event of an issue on the network, Cloudflare's systems now fail more gracefully, reducing the potential impact radius and ensuring that customer traffic is delivered even in worst-case scenarios. Product teams have reviewed their potential failure modes and implemented better failure modes, such as "fail stale" or "fail open" depending on the situation.

For example, if a failed rollout of the Bot Management detection machine learning classifier were to occur again, the system would detect the failure in an early stage of the deployment, before it had affected more than a small percentage of traffic. The system would then refuse to use the updated configuration and instead use the old configuration, or fail open to ensure customer production traffic continues to be served.

## Revised "Break Glass" and Incident Management Procedures

Cloudflare has also revised its "break glass" procedures and incident management processes to ensure that the company can respond more quickly and effectively in the event of an outage. This includes developing backup authorization pathways for key services, conducting regular drills to ensure that engineers have the necessary skills and knowledge, and establishing a dedicated communications team to work with incident responders.

## Codifying Improvements

To ensure that the lessons learned from the November and December outages are not forgotten, Cloudflare has built an internal Codex that solidifies its guidelines and best practices into clear and concise rules. The Codex is enforced through AI-powered code reviews that highlight any instances that may diverge from the guidelines, requiring additional manual reviews to be performed.

The Codex is a living repository of engineering standards written by domain experts, and it is continuously improved over time. It provides a shared knowledge base that is accessible to everyone, and it helps to enforce best practices throughout the software development lifecycle.

## Transparency and Communication

Finally, Cloudflare recognizes the importance of transparency and communication in the event of an outage. The company is committed to keeping its customers updated every step of the way, so that they can stay focused on what matters to them. This includes introducing additional service level objectives (SLOs) to all services, enforcing a global changelog, onboarding all teams to the maintenance coordination system, and improving transparency across the company on incident "prevents" ticket backlog.

By completing the Code Orange initiative, Cloudflare has taken a significant step forward in enhancing its network resilience, security, and reliability. The company remains committed to continuous improvement and to providing the highest level of service quality to its customers.
