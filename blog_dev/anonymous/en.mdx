# VPN vs Proxy: The Privacy Illusion

Many people think: *"If I use a VPN, I am completely anonymous."* 

Unfortunately, modern websites use much more than just your IP address to track you. They use browser fingerprinting, WebRTC, DNS leaks, and behavioral tracking. 

<Callout type="danger" title="The Hard Truth">
  Simply changing your IP address does **not** stop a sophisticated tracker. Your browser fingerprint often gives you away instantly.
</Callout>

## Proxy vs VPN: What's the Difference?

<Stepper>
  <Step title="Proxy: The Intermediary">
    A proxy (HTTP/SOCKS5) sits between your browser and the website. It changes your IP but usually doesn't encrypt traffic. It's often app-specific (e.g., just your browser).
  </Step>
  <Step title="VPN: The Encrypted Tunnel">
    A VPN encrypts *all* traffic leaving your device and routes it through a server. It protects you from your ISP and applies system-wide.
  </Step>
</Stepper>

### Should You Combine Them?

Yes! Advanced users often combine them, but **order matters**:
- **Proxy → VPN:** (Browser uses Proxy, OS uses VPN). Easy setup, great for hiding the proxy's IP from the destination.
- **VPN → Proxy:** Harder to route, but hides your real IP from the proxy provider itself.

## The Real Enemy: Browser Fingerprinting

Fingerprinting identifies you using screen resolution, fonts, WebGL, timezone, and installed extensions. 

<Callout type="warning" title="Stop Randomizing Everything">
  Installing 20 privacy extensions and faking your user agent often makes you **more unique**. The best privacy strategy is blending into the crowd!
</Callout>

### Common Privacy Leaks (And How to Fix Them)

1. **IPv6 Leaks:** Common on Linux. Your IPv4 might show Japan, but your IPv6 reveals your real location. Disable IPv6 in `sysctl` if your VPN doesn't support it!
2. **WebRTC Leaks:** WebRTC can expose your local and public IP. In Brave/Firefox, set WebRTC policies to disable non-proxied UDP.
3. **Timezone/Language Mismatch:** If your IP is in Tokyo but your system clock is set to New York, you look incredibly suspicious.

<Callout type="info" title="Test Your Setup">
  Don't guess! Use tools like **BrowserLeaks.com**, **IPLeak.net**, and EFF's **Cover Your Tracks** to verify your fingerprint and ensure your DNS isn't leaking.
</Callout>
