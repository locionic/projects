At the heart of SPDD lies the concept of treating prompts as first-class artifacts, stored alongside code in version control. This approach ensures that prompts are not ephemeral, but rather a permanent part of the development process. By doing so, SPDD fosters alignment between development and business needs, enabling teams to work more effectively towards common goals.

**Key Skills for Effective SPDD**
-------------------------------

To successfully implement SPDD, developers require three essential skills:

### 1. Alignment

Developers must be able to align their work with business needs, ensuring that the prompts they create accurately reflect the requirements of the project.

### 2. Abstraction-First

The ability to think in abstractions is crucial in SPDD. Developers must be able to distill complex problems into concise, well-defined prompts that can be effectively addressed by LLM programming assistants.

### 3. Iterative Review

Iterative review is a critical component of SPDD. Developers must be able to review and refine their prompts, incorporating feedback from both human teammates and LLM programming assistants.

By mastering these skills, developers can unlock the full potential of SPDD, leveraging LLM programming assistants to drive more effective collaboration and higher-quality code.
